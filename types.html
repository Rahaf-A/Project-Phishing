Types of Phishing

Phishing isn’t used to define a specific scam. The term Phishing is used as an umbrella term that covers many types of online social engineering attacks. Below, you will find four examples of phishing to look out for.

4 Types of Phishing

Spear Phishing

This type occurs when the attacker already knows some information about a specific person and uses that information to seem more legitimate. This type is the most successful since it has information and context relating to the target which makes the target believe it’s true.

Whaling

Spear phishing's subtype of whaling is typically even more precise. The distinction is that whaling targets specific people like high-ranking business executives, famous people, and wealthy people. These high-value targets' account credentials typically open the door to more information and possibly money.

Smishing

An SMS message-based phishing attack is known as smishing. Due to the notification, the victim receives and the fact that more people are likely to read text messages than emails, this kind of phishing attack is more visible. Smishing has grown in popularity as SMS messaging between customers and businesses becomes more common.
